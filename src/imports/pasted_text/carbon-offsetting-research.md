SECONDARY RESEARCH
SECONDARY RESEARCH
Carbon markets allow organizations to offset emissions by purchasing carbon credits,
where one credit represents one ton of CO₂ reduction (World Bank, 2023). 
Voluntary carbon markets are complex and mainly used by large corporations, making
them difficult for small businesses to access (Ecosystem Marketplace, 2022; Haya et al.,
2020). 
The hospitality and food service sector produces significant carbon emissions due to
energy use, transportation, and waste generation (Filimonau & De Coteau, 2019). 
Restaurants and commercial kitchens have high carbon footprints, but small businesses
often lack tools to measure and reduce emissions (Filimonau et al., 2021). 
Customers prefer environmentally responsible businesses and are more likely to support
restaurants with sustainable practices (Han & Hyun, 2018). 
Many consumers are willing to pay more for products and services that follow net-zero or
low-carbon practices (Boston Consulting Group, 2023). 
Existing carbon credit platforms are designed for enterprises and present technical
information that small business owners find difficult to use (Haya et al., 2020).
